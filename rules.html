False Show - Complete Rules

üéØ Objective

Be the last player standing by keeping your score low. Players are eliminated when they reach the agreed-upon score limit (typically 100-150 points).

üìã Game Setup

  1. Players: 4-6 players
  2. Deck: Standard 52-card deck
  3. Score Limit: Agree on an elimination score (75 for quick, 100 standard, 150 for long games)
  4. Deal:
    • 4 players: 8 cards each
    • 5 players: 7 cards each
    • 6 players: 6 cards each
  5. The Joker: Dealer reveals bottom 2 cards, picks one as the Joker (worth 0 points)

Valid Plays

  • Single: Any one card
  • Pair: Two cards of the same rank
  • Sequence: Three or more cards of the same suit in consecutive order

üé≤ Special Actions

Calling "Show!"

At any time during your turn, you can declare "Show!" if you believe you have the lowest hand value:

  • If Correct: You score 0 points, everyone else scores their hand totals
  • If Wrong: You get a 50-point penalty, the actual lowest hand scores 0

The Joker Rules

  • Acts as a wild card in any set
  • Worth 0 points in hand
  • If it's your last card: Must call "Show!" or discard it and draw a new card

üìä Scoring

Round End

A round ends when:

  • A player empties their hand (scores 0, others score hand totals)
  • Someone calls "Show!" (see above)
  • The deck runs out (all players score hand totals)

Elimination

Players are eliminated when their cumulative score reaches or exceeds the agreed limit.

Winning

The last player remaining wins the game!

üí° Strategy Tips

  • Early Game: Focus on safe plays to avoid penalties
  • Watch Patterns: Track what cards have been played
  • Strategic Penalties: Sometimes drawing is worth dumping high cards
  • Bluff Timing: Act confident even with unsafe plays
  • Show Gambit: Calculate risk vs reward carefully
  • Joker Strategy: Save it for crucial moments
